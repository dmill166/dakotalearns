# Teaching CS1 in Public

I'm building a full, free, beginner-first Computer Science 1 course — in the open — and writing about it as I go. Here's what it is and why.

<!-- more -->

## The idea

Most people who want to learn to program hit the same wall: where do I even start, and who's teaching for *me* — someone with no background and no assumptions baked in? I learned on free, open resources, and I want to pay that forward while I prepare to teach.

So I'm making **CS 1050 — Computer Science 1** the way I wish it had been handed to me:

- **No prerequisites.** No prior programming, no heavy math.
- **Portable.** A strong CS1 you'd recognize at any school, aligned to real standards (ABET, ACM/IEEE-CS CS2023) and built to set you up for the next course.
- **Honest.** Every module says what it covers, what it leaves out, and why.
- **Free.** Read it, learn from it, share it — no wall.

## Built in public

The materials live in open repositories, and I'll post here as each piece comes together — the design decisions, the things that surprised me, the teaching tradeoffs. If you're learning, you can follow along unit by unit. If you teach, you'll see the seams.
