# Teaching CS1 in Public

Why, hello there! 👋 If you're new here: I'm building a full, free, beginner-first **Computer Science 1** course out in the open — and writing about it as I go. Let me tell you what it is and, more importantly, *why*.

<!-- more -->

## The wall (and why I'm doing this)

Almost everyone who sets out to learn programming hits the same wall: *where do I even start? And who's teaching this for me* — someone with no background, no fancy math, none of the assumptions the "intro" courses quietly bake in?

I know that wall well. I learned to program on free, open resources — the kind generous people put online and asked nothing for — and I leaned on them the whole way through school and into my career. So this is me paying that forward while I prepare to teach: building the Computer Science 1 I wish someone had handed *me* on day one.

## What I'm building

Here's the shape of it:

- **No prerequisites.** No prior programming, no heavy math. If you can use a computer, you can start. (A lot of the early going will feel foreign — that's okay! Everyone starts there.)
- **Portable.** A solid CS1 you'd recognize at any school, aligned to real standards (ABET, ACM/IEEE-CS CS2023) and built to set you up for the *next* course, not just this one.
- **Honest.** Every piece says what it covers, what it leaves out, and why. No hand-waving.
- **Free.** Read it, learn from it, share it — you'll never hit a wall.

## "Built in public" — what does that even mean?

Glad you asked! It means I'm not disappearing for six months and reappearing with a polished course. I'm building it where you can watch — posting here as each piece comes together: the design decisions, the things that surprise me, the teaching trade-offs I'm wrestling with. If you're learning, you can follow along unit by unit. If you teach, you'll see the seams — and honestly, I'd love for you to tell me where I can do better.

Think of it less like a finished textbook dropped on your desk, and more like building a house with the walls still open — you get to see the wiring.
